Challenge

Challenges

Keeping the Design Consistent Across Many Teams

TaipeiPASS connects many different services in one place. Many outside companies helped build it. Each company had their own designers. Without shared design rules, the platform looked and felt different in each section.

Specific Problems

  • Each company made their own design
  • Too many different UI parts were created
  • The platform did not look or feel consistent
  • It was hard to check quality when adding new features
Solution

Updating and Using the Design Guidelines

Design Guidelines: A Shared Foundation for Multi-Vendor Work

TaipeiPASS is a large platform where many outside vendors build features at the same time. Each vendor has their own designers and engineers. Without shared design rules, it's very hard to keep the UI consistent across the whole platform.

The Design Guideline was the shared foundation that kept all vendors building in the same direction.

At first, the guidelines covered colors, font sizes, and spacing. As the platform grew, the UX team rebuilt them around "user behavior" — not just how things look, but how users interact. This made the guidelines more useful as a real design standard, not just a style guide. We published them through Adobe XD so all vendors could access them at any time.

How the Guidelines Were Used

  • Served as the shared design language for all vendors — a bridge between UI design and development
  • Every new vendor or feature team used the guidelines as a reference before starting work
  • When questions came up during development, the guidelines gave a clear basis for decisions
  • As a UX team member, I helped update the guidelines and supported vendors in applying them correctly

Applying the Guidelines in Real Projects

I used these guidelines as the design standard for every feature I worked on. After development, I also did QA checks to make sure the final build matched the design intent.

Results & Impact

Results & Learnings

Project Results

  • The guidelines became the shared standard for all vendors — keeping UI quality consistent across the whole platform
  • New vendors could get started faster because the design rules were clear and accessible
  • Fewer design inconsistencies between teams, which reduced back-and-forth and rework during development

What I Learned

  • I learned how to build and use design guidelines for a large platform
  • I learned how to work with many different people in a government project
  • As the senior designer, I presented screen designs to managers and leaders
  • Good teamwork makes the quality of design much better

Reflection

Working on a large platform taught me how hard it is to keep the design consistent. The design guidelines and QA process were the most important tools for this.

Our PM was excellent, and the team worked very well together. It was a great experience to work as one strong team.
