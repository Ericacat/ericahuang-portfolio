BizCHINA
Learning Platform

I led the full redesign of a business Chinese learning platform — from onboarding and design system to frontend rebuild and platform integration.
The work improved sign-up rates, reduced development costs, and built a system that could scale to new products.

Role
Product Designer
UI/UX + Frontend
Period
Feb 2024 – Present
Company
Netstar Inc.
Team Size
6 people (small team)
Challenge

Challenges

1. The outdated brand was hurting sign-ups

The old site looked out of date compared to modern online learning platforms. This was hurting the brand's first impression and making it harder to get new users to sign up.

2. The old system was slowing down product growth

The original system had too much technical debt. Every time we added a new feature, it took more time and money than it should. The old architecture was holding the product back.

3. Too many separate tools were making operations messy

Classes, teacher communication, and attendance tracking all used different outside tools. Information was scattered, and it was hard to get a clear picture of how the platform was running. Bringing everything into one place was a priority.

Solution 1

Brand Rebuild & Better Sign-Up Flow

The main problems were trust and friction — users weren't confident enough to sign up. I worked on the design system and the onboarding flow at the same time to fix both.

1-1. Design System

Instead of just updating the look, I built a design system that could support future features and new products — not just the current redesign.

  • Rebuilt the visual style (colors, fonts, components) to make the platform look trustworthy and modern
  • Organized design components so adding new features takes less design work
  • Built a shared foundation that made it possible to launch NetKOREA later

1-2. Onboarding Flow Redesign

The Problem

After the redesign, more users started free trials. But not many became paying members. The 5-step sign-up process was too long — users were dropping out before they finished.

What Each Stakeholder Wanted

Three groups had different goals. My job was to find a solution that worked for all of them.

  • Management: Show the price upfront, so only serious users move forward
  • Internal staff: Make sure users get enough information before signing up
  • Users: Start quickly without too many steps

What I Did

  • Reduced from 5 steps to 2 to remove the main drop-off points
  • Put pricing on the first step — users who continue already know the cost
  • Moved detailed information to after sign-up — less to think about at the start
  • Proposed switching from a free trial to a low-cost paid trial — this naturally filtered out users who weren't serious

Results

✅ Paid sign-ups increased steadily. Onboarding-related support questions dropped a lot.
✅ The paid trial change brought in more committed users and reduced early drop-off.

Solution 2

Frontend Rebuild & System Improvements

Our team of 6 had no dedicated frontend engineer, so I handled both design and development. This turned out to be a real advantage — the final product matched the design exactly, with no back-and-forth between teams.

Why Doing Both Design and Development Helped

  • No gap between design and the final result
  • No need for design QA back-and-forth
  • Much faster from decision to shipped feature
  • Easy to update the design quickly based on user feedback

Case 1: One Codebase for All Devices (Responsive Design)

Problem

The old site had two separate versions — one for desktop and one for mobile. Every change had to be done twice. Maintenance was taking up more and more time.

What I Did

I rebuilt the frontend with Vue.js using a responsive design, so one codebase works on all devices. I planned the breakpoints alongside the visual design to make sure everything matched.

Results

  • No more duplicate development — maintenance became much simpler
  • One codebase made it easier to plan and scope new features
  • Better experience for users on mobile

Case 2: Building Our Own Web Classroom

Problem

We used outside video tools for classes. This meant attendance data and class information were stored outside our platform. Operations had no clear view of what was happening.

What I Did

  • Designed and built our own web classroom interface
  • Connected the video system with a simple, easy-to-use UI
  • Brought class data back into the platform so the team could monitor it in real time

Results

  • Class and attendance data is now all in one place — the team has a much clearer view
  • Reduced costs from outside tools and made managing lessons more efficient
Solution 3

Instructor WeChat Mini Program

Problem

Teachers had to use many different tools just to check their schedule, find materials, or see student progress. It was slow and easy to miss things.

What I Did

  • Designed and built a WeChat Mini Program for teachers using Vue.js + uni-app
  • Brought schedule, materials, and student progress into one mobile interface
  • Reorganized how course data was shown, so it matched how teachers actually work

Results

✅ Teachers now manage everything in one place — much less work and fewer mistakes
✅ Teachers said it made their daily workflow much easier
✅ Fewer missed sessions or scheduling errors

Results & Impact

Results & Impact

Business & Product

  • Sign-up rate increased steadily after the onboarding redesign. Support inquiries dropped a lot.
  • New features now take less time to ship after the frontend rebuild
  • One codebase removed the need to maintain two separate versions
  • Bringing tools in-house reduced outside tool costs and made operations more efficient

System & Team

  • The design system made it possible to launch NetKOREA without starting from scratch
  • Owning both design and development helped the small team move fast
  • Having all data in one platform made it easier for the team to make decisions

Platform Expansion: NetKOREA

The system and design system built for BizCHINA were used to launch NetKOREA, a Korean language learning platform. We reused the core platform and redesigned the experience for Korean learners. This showed that the same architecture could support multiple language products.

AI-Assisted Design Workflow

I used AI tools — including Claude — to speed up prototyping, organize requirements, and prepare for stakeholder meetings. This helped me move faster from design to a spec that developers could work with. In a small team, it made a real difference in how quickly we could iterate.
